Applied Differential Privacy is a hands-on course about learning from sensitive data without losing sight of the people represented in it. Students examine privacy attacks, derive differential privacy through hypothesis testing, audit claimed guarantees, and design useful private methods for statistics, machine learning, and synthetic data.

The course moves from attacks and rigorous definitions to private statistics, private machine learning, and synthetic data. Lectures pair narrative slides with self-study notes and live, in-browser experiments.
